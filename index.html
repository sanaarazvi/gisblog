A Case Study of Hyderabad, India

Urban Flood Monitoring Using QGIS

Urban flooding has become a serious environmental problem in rapidly growing cities like Hyderabad. The combination of heavy rainfall events, unplanned urban expansion, and inadequate drainage systems has significantly increased the frequency and intensity of flooding in recent years. Hyderabad has experienced several extreme rainfall events that caused waterlogging, property damage, and disruption of daily life. The loss of natural drainage channels, lakes, and green spaces due to urbanization has reduced the city’s ability to absorb excess rainwater. This study uses Geographic Information System (GIS) technology, specifically QGIS, to analyze and identify flood-prone areas within the city. Various spatial datasets such as rainfall data, Digital Elevation Model (DEM), land use and land cover, and drainage networks were integrated for analysis. Through spatial analysis techniques such as overlay analysis and terrain modeling, low-lying regions and highly vulnerable urban zones were identified. These areas are mostly located near water bodies, drainage channels, and densely populated settlements. The results clearly show that urban expansion without proper planning has increased surface runoff and reduced natural infiltration. GIS-based flood mapping provides an effective way to support urban planning, disaster management, and sustainable development in rapidly growing cities.
